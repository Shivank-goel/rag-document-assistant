In modern enterprise systems, batch job processing plays a crucial role in handling large volumes of data. Batch jobs are typically scheduled to run at specific intervals and are responsible for tasks such as data aggregation, report generation, and system synchronization. Ensuring the reliability and performance of batch jobs is essential to avoid data inconsistencies and system downtime.

Machine learning and artificial intelligence are increasingly being adopted to improve enterprise software systems. AI-driven monitoring can detect anomalies in batch processing workflows, predict system failures, and recommend corrective actions before issues impact business operations.

Large Language Models (LLMs) have emerged as powerful tools for processing unstructured text data. They can be used for tasks such as document summarization, question answering, and intelligent search. However, LLMs may generate incorrect or fabricated responses when they lack sufficient context.

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) addresses this challenge by combining information retrieval with text generation. Instead of relying solely on the model’s internal knowledge, RAG systems retrieve relevant documents and provide them as context to the LLM, resulting in more accurate and grounded responses.

In a typical RAG pipeline, documents are ingested, cleaned, and split into smaller chunks. These chunks are converted into embeddings and stored in a vector database. When a user submits a query, the system retrieves the most relevant chunks based on semantic similarity and uses them to generate a response.

Designing an effective RAG system requires careful consideration of chunk size, overlap, embedding models, and retrieval strategies. Proper evaluation and monitoring are necessary to ensure the system produces reliable and high-quality answers.
